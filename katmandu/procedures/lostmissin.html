Procedure for recording replacement decisions for missing and lost books

Collection development staff regularly review titles that are marked missing or lost-library applied and make decisions to replace or to not replace missing titles. These decisions are recorded in the holdings record for the lost/missing copy as detailed below.

Step 1. Selector notified of missing/lost copy as a result of:

  • Circulation points generating notice of a lost or missing item to selector either as a result of a trace request or an interlibrary loan request.
  • Systems Office producing report of items with status of missing or lost-library applied with a status date greater than the last report date.

Step 2. Selector makes decision to:

  • Replace missing copy. Forwards request to Order Division for action (Step 3).
  • Not to replace missing copy. Forwards decision to Catalog Division, Holdings and Record Management Unit (Step 4).

Step 3. Order Division:

  • Updates MFHD record for missing copy to include note in 852 field:

Step 4. Catalog Division, Holdings and Record Management Unit:

  • Check item status to confirm missing/lost status. Copies no longer missing are not updated.

Suppress the BIB record if only copy. Suppress the MFHD if more than one copy.

Circulation staff:

Discharges items that have been marked missing or lost/library applied and updates item status to reflect current condition.

Systems staff:

Runs regular monthly reports to identify records with suppressed status, either BIB or MFHD, where discharge date post-dates the date the record was suppressed.

Forwards that report to Cat HARMS who will mark record to display in OPAC, (and do what about the note?)

Runs periodic (annual, bi-annual?) reports to identify records with Desiderata note in MFHD and no active order.

          Forwards report to Order Division to search for out of print copies.
