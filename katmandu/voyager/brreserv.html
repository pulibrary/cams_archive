Guidelines for bibliographic information in brief records for Reserve

The material covered includes photocopies, course packets, professor's copies, handouts and other uncataloged library material. The following standards should be followed for each category of material:

NOTE: If there is a matching bibliographic record in Voyager, add a holdings record for the reserve copy according to Adding Holdings and Item records to Voyager for monographs and booksets procedure or Adding Holdings and Item records to Voyager for serials procedure. Make a note in the item record note field that this is a personal copy.

1. Xerox of Journal article:
In bib record
100 1   Baumol, W
245 1 0 Analysis of macroeconomics
590     Journal of Economics, vol. 30, 1997, pp. 267-286.

OR
100 1   Baumol, W
245 1 0 Analysis of macroeconomics ...: Journal of Economics, vol. 30, 1997, pp. 267-286

In MFHD record
852 8    X ECO 311 BAU

2. Xerox from book:

In bib record
110 2   World Bank
245 1 0 Unleashing markets
590     Ch. 3 of "Adjustment in Africa" or
"p. 200-225 of "Adjustment in Africa"

OR
110 2   World Bank
245 1 0 Unleashing markets : Ch. 3 of "Adjustment in Africa" or "p. 200-225 of "Adjustment in Africa"

In MFHD record
852 8   X ECO 321 WOR

3. Packets of articles:

In bib record
245 0 0 Packet of readings for ECO 325

In MFHD record
852 8   X ECO 325 PAC

4. Professor's personal copy of monograph:

In bib record
100 1   Schulz, D.
245 1 0 Whither Haiti

In MFHD record
852 8    X ECO 321 SCH

In Item record
     In the item note "Quill pen box" add note Return to prof. at end of semester.

5. Call numbers for reserve items should follow this pattern:

Call numbers should have a capital X--space--the first three letters of the course (in caps)--space--the number of the course--space--the first three letters of the author's name if present, otherwise the first three letters of the title (in caps). Use the first 3 letter course designation from the Princeton University Course Offerings.

If the three last letters in a call number conflict add a number directly after the letters to differentiate between titles.

Examples:
X ECO 325 PAC
X ECO 325 PAC1
X ECO 325 PAC2
