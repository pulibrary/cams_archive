Cataloging Biblical Materials

Psalters

In many instances the uniform title of a Psalter is qualified by the authorized form of a manuscript. This is because most published editions of the Psalter are facsimiles of medieval manuscripts.

The current liturgy of the Catholic Church does not have the Psalter per se, rather these manuscripts were produced when the Psalter was a separate book.

When a facsimile of a Psalter is published, the cataloger must determine whether the intention of the publication is to present a liturgical text or a work of art. If the Psalter being cataloged is already in the NAF, look for a note such as the following:

Heading [for Psalter as a liturgical book] represents the textual content of ms.; for ms. as a physical entity, including its decoration, use ... [uniform title for ms.].
Such a note would only be given when both the liturgical text and the manuscript (or facsimile) are 130 0 headings in the NAF, in which case each would be a 530 0 for the other.

When that is not the case, you need to determine whether the item in hand is published as a liturgical text or work of art.

  • One helpful hint is to look at the subtitle (if there is one). If it gives liturgical details, it is a liturgical text and would have the Church as the main entry.
