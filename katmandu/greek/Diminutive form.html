The forms of Male fore- and sur-names were discussed on the Personal Names page of this site, where the rule of thumb for determining male gender was the presence of a final “s” in the Nominative (subject) case.

In Greece, a diminutive form of first names is quite common.  You may encounter variant forms of first names in their shortened form.  If an author’s name appears on the title page in its abbreviated form, it may be difficult to determine gender or to know the full form of the name (should the author have published using the fuller form).  An excellent book on the subject of Greek first names is Ta Neoellēnika kyria onomata (Theodōros Seremetakēs, Maria Dēmētriou : Athēna : Ekdoseis Patakē, 2003).  On this page, you will find a summary and list of the most common forenames, male and female, with their corresponding abbreviated forms compiled from the book

As is the case with Greek surnames, the diminutive, or abbreviated form of forenames, once studied, follows a fairly predictable order.

The two most common masculine diminutives are : (p. 26)
 Akēs  - short for: Giōrgakēs, Dēmētrakēs, Thodōrakēs
 
Akos  - short for: Vasilakos, Thodōrakos, Kyriakos 
     To name a few.

The suffix ending  –erēs  is often the diminutive form of male forenames ending in –os
