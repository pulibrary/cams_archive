General criteria for inclusion on the Library's Electronic Journals web page:

  • Title must be "selected" – either a selector has specifically ordered the e-version, or the e-version is automatically included with a print subscription, or the title is included in a package to which the Library subscribes. For titles that are freely available on the Web, either a selector must request cataloging, or we must also have its print counterpart cataloged.
  • Content must be full text, not just abstracts or tables of contents, and not just selected issues/sections. (A very few exceptions have been made.)
  • Access must be expected to be somewhat stable and ongoing, and a stable URL must be available. Trial subscriptions are listed on a different web page. Titles included in large aggregator packages (e.g. ProQuest), which are notorious for continually losing & gaining content, are not listed.
  • Title generally should be of "journal" type – i.e., a periodical or an annual, NOT a newspaper or a monographic series. Working papers, conference proceedings, and annual reports, though their e-versions may be reflected in the catalog, generally are not added to the e-j page. (A very few exceptions have been made.)
